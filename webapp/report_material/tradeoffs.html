Design Trade-offs

Key engineering decisions and their rationale

1 Polling frequency
Decision
1/min over 5/min
Why
TfL updates station data approximately every 5 minutes, but the exact timing is unknown. Polling every minute ensures no update is missed and provides finer granularity for the lag feature used by the nowcast model.
Trade-off: 5x more storage and API calls. ~315k rows in 16 days vs ~63k at 5/min.
2 Two models vs one
Decision
Separate nowcast + forecast models
Why
The lag feature (empty_docks_lag1) delivers a 3x accuracy improvement but is only available in real-time. A single model would either sacrifice accuracy (no lag) or be unusable for future planning (requires lag).
Trade-off: Added architectural complexity. Two model files, two API endpoints, two training pipelines to maintain.
3 Historical Average vs ML for Forecast
Decision
Historical Average baseline
Why
With only ~16 days of training data, ML models (Random Forest, Gradient Boosting) could not learn robust weather-dock relationships. The simpler baseline — mean docks by station, hour, and weekday — generalised better and was more interpretable.
Trade-off: Loses ability to capture weather effects on dock availability. With more data (months), ML would likely surpass the baseline.
4 Open-Meteo vs OpenWeatherMap
Decision
Open-Meteo
Why
No API key required, unlimited calls, and equivalent data quality for temperature, precipitation, humidity, and wind speed. OpenWeatherMap imposes a 1,000 calls/day limit on the free tier and requires key management.
Trade-off: Open-Meteo has less granular forecast data and no severe weather alerts. Acceptable for this use case.
5 Supabase vs MongoDB Atlas
Decision
Supabase PostgreSQL
Why
SQL joins are essential for merging bike and weather time-series on minute-level timestamps. PostgreSQL's TIMESTAMPTZ type handles timezone-aware queries natively. Free tier (500 MB) sufficient for the project scope.
Trade-off: Rigid schema requires migrations for structural changes. MongoDB's flexible documents would simplify schema evolution.
6 Heroku vs Raspberry Pi
Decision
Heroku (cloud)
Why
Reliable 24/7 uptime critical for continuous data collection. No hardware maintenance, no dependency on home network. GitHub Student Pack provides free dyno hours. Separate worker and web dynos for clean separation of concerns.
Trade-off: Higher long-term cost after student credits expire. Raspberry Pi would be a one-time ~$35 investment but requires physical setup, static IP or tunneling, and is vulnerable to power/network outages.
